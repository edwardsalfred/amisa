Executive Summary

The state of the market in four sentences

The Houston accent wall and interior wall design market is underdeveloped from a digital marketing standpoint — competitors rely on template websites, lack strong photography, and have no clear brand differentiation. The top local competitor (CK Custom Wall Design) is built on Wix with limited SEO control. The best-designed site in the niche (Accent Wall Designs) is based in Arizona, leaving a clear gap for a premium, locally-positioned Houston brand. AmiSa has a structural SEO advantage with 5 dedicated landing pages and an active Instagram presence — the new website must convert that raw traffic potential into booked consultations through better design, storytelling, and a live gallery experience.

Patterns of the Top 10%

What the best sites in this niche consistently do that the rest don't

1

Immediate Value Proposition Above the Fold

The best sites communicate what they do, who for, and why them — in under 8 seconds. AmiSa's current JS-rendered hero delays this. The new site needs a hero that loads instantly with a bold headline, strong photography, and a clear CTA visible without scrolling.

2

Simple 3–4 Step Process Section

Every strong competitor shows a "How It Works" section. Interior design clients are anxious about the process — showing a clear path from consultation to completed room dramatically reduces friction. "Consultation → Design → Install → Enjoy" is the formula.

3

Categorized Gallery with Premium Photography

The gallery is the #1 decision driver for this service type. Best-in-class sites organize by room type (Bedroom / Living Room / Office / Kids Room) and use large, full-bleed photography. AmiSa's Instagram is the perfect source — a live-connected feed makes the gallery self-updating and proves active business.

4

Social Proof Near the Top, Not Buried

Testimonials and star ratings should appear in the first 2 sections, not the footer. None of the competitors in this market do this well — it's a clear differentiator. Google review embeds, client photos, and before/after images build instant trust.

5

Friction-Free Mobile Contact

A sticky "Get Free Quote" button on mobile, a one-field contact form (name + phone), and a direct call link. The majority of interior design searches happen on phones. The site that makes it easiest to contact gets the booking. No competitor in this market has optimized for this.

05

Recommended Design Direction

Backed by competitor data — not guesswork

Refined Color Palette

Keep AmiSa's gold brand color, deepen the dark tones, add a warm cream for premium texture.

Dark black → rich brown → AmiSa gold → light gold → warm cream → white

Typography

Headings: Cormorant Garamond — editorial, luxury, timeless
Body: DM Sans — clean, modern, readable
Accent: Keep Tajawal for brand continuity

Photography Style

Warm-toned, editorial interiors. Full-bleed room shots. Before/after pairs. Owner in action. Natural light preferred. Instagram feed provides live content.

Animations

GSAP ScrollTrigger for section reveals. Parallax on hero image. Gallery fade-in on scroll. Smooth page transitions. No heavy animations — cinematic, not circus.

What to Avoid

Wix/template aesthetic. Exaggerated claims. Cluttered layouts. Auto-playing video with sound. Tiny mobile CTAs. JS-blocked above-the-fold content.

Instagram Auto-Sync Gallery — Recommended

AmiSa's Instagram page (@amisa.interiordesign) is an existing content engine. Rather than manually maintaining a gallery, embedding a live Instagram feed widget (via Behold.so or Elfsight) means every new Instagram post automatically appears on the website gallery — zero manual work required. The feed can be filtered by room type using hashtags (#amisabedroom, #amisalivingroom, etc.). This is a significant competitive advantage no Houston competitor currently has.


Recommended tool: Behold.so (free tier available, no Instagram app approval required for basic feeds, updates automatically)
